## 🛠️ Getting started

Open the application by double-clicking the icon. You will see a login dashboard.

1. Create your profile by entering your name and email address. 
2. Link your existing notification tools. The app connects to email, SMS, and messaging platforms to send you alerts.
3. Define your on-call schedule. Add team members by entering their contact details.
4. Set the rules that trigger an automatic alert. You can define specific keywords from your monitoring tools to start an incident response.

## 🧠 How the AI works

The application uses intelligence to group repetitive alerts. Instead of receiving ten notifications for the same issue, the software identifies the pattern. It sends a single summary report that includes the likely cause and the history of recent changes. This approach reduces noise and helps you focus on high-priority tasks.

## 🛡️ Managing incidents

When an incident starts, the software creates a new ticket. It displays the severity level, the systems affected, and the current on-call engineer.

- Acknowledge the incident. This stops further alerts for that specific issue.
- View the incident timeline. The app logs every event from the moment it detects a problem.
- Resolve the incident. Closing the ticket updates your team and marks the issue as finished.

## 🔌 Connecting your tools

You can connect the application to the services your team uses every day:

- Monitoring services: Track CPU, memory, and disk usage.
- Networking tools: Detect outages or high latency.
- Messaging apps: Receive alerts directly in your team channels.
- Cloud platforms: Monitor your virtual machines and servers.

Navigate to "Settings" then "Integrations" to see a list of supported services. Click "Add" next to the service you want to connect. You will see instructions for entering your API keys or account tokens. 

## 📈 Improving your team's process

The software tracks your incident history over time. Look at the "Analytics" tab to see your response times. 

- Response Time: How long it takes for someone to acknowledge a new alert.
- Resolution Time: How long it takes to solve the issue.
- Alert Frequency: Which services trigger the most incidents.

Use this data to identify weak points in your infrastructure. If a specific service triggers many alerts, it may need hardware updates or a change in configuration.

## ❓ Frequently asked questions

**Does the software store my data in the cloud?**
No. The application runs locally on your Windows machine. Your incident data and contact information stay on your disk.

**Can I use this without internet access?**
The software needs an internet connection to send and receive alerts from your connected services.

**How do I update the application?**
Check the releases page once a month. Download the newest installer and run it. The installer automatically replaces the older version while keeping your configuration files intact.

**Can I add more than one team?**
Yes. Create multiple schedules within the app if your organization manages different services.

**Is it safe to run on my work computer?**
The software is designed for use by professional teams. It respects your local permissions and operates under the user account you designate during installation.

## 🤝 Getting extra help

If you encounter a problem, check the log files located in the installation folder. You can also view the GitHub repository on the web to see if other users have reported similar issues.
